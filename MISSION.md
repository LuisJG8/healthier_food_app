# Mission: Strategic Programming

## Why
AI is making tactical implementation cheaper, so the valuable skill is deciding what should be built, why it matters, how to de-risk it, and how to keep the system coherent as implementation accelerates. The learning path should help turn BetterBite from "an app with code" into a product and codebase shaped by explicit bets, constraints, feedback loops, and durable design choices.

## Success looks like
- Frame a feature as a user bet, system bet, data bet, and release bet before coding.
- Decompose large work into small, reviewable slices that still preserve the product thesis.
- Design QA around the riskiest assumptions rather than only around implementation details.
- Use AI as an execution multiplier while retaining ownership of architecture, tradeoffs, and product judgment.

## Constraints
- Lessons should be short, concrete, and tied to this workspace.
- Exercises should use BetterBite examples whenever possible.
- The product direction is healthier swaps that preserve craving, format, and eating occasion.

## Out of scope
- Generic motivational career advice.
- Pure syntax, framework, or tool tutorials unless they serve a strategic decision.
- Broad management training unrelated to technical product and codebase judgment.
