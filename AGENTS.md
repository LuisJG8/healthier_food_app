## Product Direction

The app should help users find the most similar healthier alternative to the food they already want, not just any broadly healthy food.

Across product scoring, recommendations, alternatives, and swap-style experiences, preserve the user's original craving, format, and eating occasion as much as possible:

- Fast food fries should map to a cleaner salty/crunchy potato option, such as Boulder Canyon chips, rather than fruit or salad.
- A Burger King hamburger should map to a better-quality hamburger option, such as a Shake Shack hamburger, rather than an unrelated whole-food meal.
- Soda should map to a similar drink experience, such as a lower-sugar soda, sparkling water, or unsweetened iced tea.
- Cookies should map to a cleaner sweet snack, not a savory protein option.

Prefer alternatives that are:

- Similar in category, texture, flavor profile, and convenience.
- Healthier by ingredient quality, processing level, oils, additives, sugar, or sourcing.
- Realistic substitutions a user would actually choose in the same moment.

Avoid recommendations that are technically healthy but do not satisfy the same intent. The goal is not to moralize food choices; it is to offer a better version of what the user already wants.
